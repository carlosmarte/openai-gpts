# Channel Style Guide — Slack, LinkedIn, Email

This guide is the source of truth for tone, structure, and length norms across the three output channels. When the system instructions and this guide conflict, this guide wins.

## Cross-Channel Principles

1. **Same insight, different shape.** All three posts must trace to the same Insight Extract. Never introduce a fact in the LinkedIn post that does not appear in the Slack post or Email brief (and vice versa).
2. **Compress, do not invent.** When tightening for length, drop bullets — do not invent specificity (no fake numbers, dates, or quotes).
3. **Source over style.** If the source is thin, the post is thin. Do not pad to hit length norms.
4. **Informational tone.** Neutral, concrete, plain language. Avoid hype, hedging, and salesy phrasing.

---

## Slack

### Purpose
High signal-to-noise. The reader is in a busy channel and decides in 3 seconds whether to keep reading.

### Structure

```
*TL;DR:* <one sentence — the single most important takeaway>

- 📊 **<bold keyword>**: <fact or insight, 1 line>
- **<bold keyword>**: <fact or insight, 1 line>
- **Caveat:** <limitation, if material>

*Action:* <one concrete next step — only if the source implies one>
```

### Rules

- **Length:** ≤ 120 words.
- **Bolding:** Lead each bullet with a 1–3 word bolded keyword. Reader scans bolds first.
- **Emoji policy:** 1–2 max, only as signal (📊 for data, 📌 for pinned takeaways, 🔍 for findings, ⚠️ for caveats). Never as decoration.
- **Action line:** Include only if the source implies a concrete next step. Do not invent.
- **Mentions:** Add `@channel` or `@here` only if the user explicitly asks. Default off.

### Anti-patterns

- "Hey team! 🚀🚀" / "Quick share 👀" — too informal, costs trust.
- Long unbroken paragraphs — Slack is a bullet medium.
- Generic "Check it out" CTAs — say what to do, not "look at this."

## LinkedIn

### Purpose
Engagement-driven but professional. The reader is scrolling a feed; the first line either earns a tap-to-expand or doesn't.

### Structure

```
<hook — 1 line, maximally specific>

<paragraph 1 — 1–2 sentences setting context>

<paragraph 2 — 2–3 sentences on the key insight>

<paragraph 3 — 1–2 sentences on benefit or implication>

<paragraph 4 — open question to invite comments>

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

### Rules

- **Length:** 150–250 words.
- **Hook taxonomy** (pick one per post):
  1. **Question hook** — "Why do 60% of onboarding programs fail in the first 30 days?"
  2. **Contrarian hook** — "The standard advice on PDF parsing is wrong for layout-heavy docs."
  3. **Surprising-stat hook** — "After 90 days, support tickets fell 22%. The variable that moved them surprised me."
- **White space:** Blank line between every paragraph. Walls of text get scrolled past.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5 lowercase, topic-specific. Avoid generic `#ai #innovation #futureofwork` — prefer `#documentintelligence #productresearch`.
- **Closing question:** Always end with one open question. Don't ask yes/no questions.

### Anti-patterns

- "🚀 Game-changer alert!" / "I'm humbled to share…" / "Thoughts?" — filler, low engagement.
- Em-dash-heavy "thread" formatting — looks AI-generated.
- More than 5 hashtags — looks spammy.
- Self-promotion language ("DM me to learn more").

## Email

### Purpose
Executive-style. The reader is busy and needs to act on or file the message in under 60 seconds.

### Structure

```
Subject: <specific, ≤ 60 chars>

Hi <name>,

<Summary — 2–3 sentences. State the source, the central thesis, and why it matters.>

**Key benefits:**
- <benefit 1>
- <benefit 2>
- <benefit 3>

**Next research areas:**
- <question 1>
- <question 2>
- <question 3>

<Optional 1-line close stating next step or willingness to discuss.>

— [Your name]
```

### Rules

- **Length:** 200–350 words total.
- **Subject line:** Specific, no clickbait, no "FW:" / "Re:" / all-caps. ≤ 60 chars. Lead with the topic, not the emotion.
- **Summary:** Lead with the source and thesis, not "I came across an interesting article."
- **Bullets:** Both lists are parallel — every benefit phrased as a noun phrase, every research area phrased as a question.
- **Tone:** Professional, declarative. No "I think" / "perhaps" / "just wanted to share."
- **Sign-off:** Include the placeholder `— [Your name]` so the user fills it in.

### Anti-patterns

- Subject lines like "Quick thought" / "FYI" / "Important!!" — unclear or inflated.
- Multi-page emails — if the source justifies 600 words, attach the source and brief in 300.
- Unstructured prose in place of bullet lists — busy readers don't parse paragraphs.

## When Content Is Thin

If the source has fewer than 3 supportable findings:

- **Slack:** Drop bullets to match. A 1-bullet TL;DR is fine.
- **LinkedIn:** Skip the post entirely or note "the source is exploratory, not conclusive" in paragraph 2.
- **Email:** Keep the structure but compress to 150 words. Note source thinness in the summary.

Never pad to hit length norms with invented specificity.
