Strategy Meeting — Q3 Platform Direction
April 3, 2026 / 2:00 PM / 90 min / Conference Room B + Zoom

Attendees: Dana (VP Product), Raj (CTO), Kenji (Eng Director), Lisa (Head of CS), Mike (Head of Sales), Anna (Design Lead)

---

Dana opened by saying Q2 numbers are tracking 12% below forecast. Main churn driver is enterprise clients leaving for competitors with better integrations. Need to decide: do we double down on our integration ecosystem or invest in the AI features clients keep asking about?

Raj presented data:
- 40% of churned accounts cited "missing integrations" in exit surveys
- Top requested: Salesforce, HubSpot, Jira, Slack (we only have Slack today)
- AI feature requests are growing but mostly from mid-market, not enterprise
- Building a proper integration platform would take 2 engineers ~4 months
- AI copilot MVP could ship in 6 weeks with the team we have

Mike pushed hard for integrations: "Every deal I lose, integrations come up. AI is nice-to-have, integrations are table stakes." Said he has 3 enterprise prospects ($500K+ combined ARR) who are waiting on Salesforce integration specifically.

Lisa agreed integrations are the priority. Said support tickets about missing integrations are up 35% QoQ.

Kenji argued we could do both if we hire 2 more engineers, but Raj said headcount is frozen until August. Kenji then suggested we build a lightweight integration framework first (Zapier-style webhooks + OAuth connector pattern) that lets us ship connectors faster — estimated 6 weeks for the framework, then 1-2 weeks per connector.

Anna raised a concern: "If we build integrations without rethinking the data model, we'll have the same mapping problems HubSpot has. We should invest a sprint in data modeling before we start building connectors."

Dana's decision: Integrations are Q3 priority #1. AI copilot moves to Q4. She wants Kenji's framework approach but with Anna's data modeling sprint upfront.

Action items people mentioned:
- Kenji will write a technical design doc for the integration framework by next Friday
- Anna will do a data model audit and propose changes — said she needs 2 weeks
- Mike will get LOIs or written commitments from the 3 enterprise prospects to validate the investment
- Lisa will compile the full list of requested integrations ranked by frequency
- Raj will re-open the headcount conversation with finance for August — wants 2 backend engineers specifically
- Dana wants a checkpoint meeting in 3 weeks to review Kenji and Anna's work before committing to build

Other topics:
- Mike mentioned a competitor (Acme Corp) just launched an AI assistant and it's getting buzz on LinkedIn — wants to make sure we don't fall too far behind
- Lisa asked if we could do a webinar for existing clients about the integration roadmap to reduce churn proactively — Dana said good idea, Lisa should own it
- Someone brought up that our API rate limits are too low for integration use cases — Raj said he'd look into it but no commitment on timeline
