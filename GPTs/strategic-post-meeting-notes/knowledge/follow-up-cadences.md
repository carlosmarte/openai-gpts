## 1. Cadence Selection Guide

| Meeting Type | Recommended Cadence | Rationale |
|-------------|---------------------|-----------|
| Status Update | Internal 1-3-7 | Maintain execution momentum with lightweight check-ins |
| Strategy Session | Internal 1-3-7 | Decisions and action items need reinforcement to stick |
| Kickoff | Internal 1-3-7 (extended) | New projects need early momentum; add a Day 14 full review |
| Retrospective | Internal 1-3-7 (improvement focus) | Improvement items easily forgotten without spaced reinforcement |
| 1:1 | Internal 1-3-7 (simplified) | Day 1 summary + Day 7 review at next 1:1 |
| Client/External Meeting | External Pulse | Value-first approach maintains relationship without pressure |
| Sales/Prospect Meeting | Sales Cadence | Structured persistence with value drops to maintain deal momentum |
| Webinar/Event | External Pulse | Capture fleeting post-event engagement before 20% daily drop-off |
| Training Session | Internal 1-3-7 (learning focus) | Spaced repetition maximizes knowledge retention |

## 2. Internal: 1-3-7 Spaced Repetition Cadence

Based on cognitive science: the human brain rapidly discards new, unreinforced information. Reviewing at increasing intervals (1 day, 3 days, 7 days) exponentially increases retention vs. single-session review.

### Day-by-Day Schedule

| Day | Offset | Action | Channel | Owner | Purpose |
|-----|--------|--------|---------|-------|---------|
| Day 1 | Meeting date + 1 | Distribute meeting summary + action items | Email + Slack/Teams | Meeting organizer | Establish the record; start the accountability clock |
| Day 3 | Meeting date + 3 | Async check-in: "Quick check on action items. Any blockers?" | Slack/Teams channel | Meeting organizer or project lead | Early blocker detection without scheduling a meeting |
| Day 7 | Meeting date + 7 | Review open action items (5-minute kick-off at next sync) | Next recurring meeting | Meeting facilitator | Social accountability; public progress review |

### Variations by Meeting Type

**For Kickoffs (extended):**
- Add Day 14: Full progress review meeting (30 min) to assess whether the project is on track
- Add Day 30: First milestone check — are initial deliverables landing?

**For Training Sessions (learning focus):**
- Day 1: Distribute training materials + summary of key concepts
- Day 3: Quiz or self-assessment on core material
- Day 7: Application exercise — ask participants to apply one concept to their current work

**For 1:1s (simplified):**
- Day 1: Share action items via DM or shared doc
- Day 7: Review at next 1:1 session (first agenda item)

### Automated Reminder Integration

Configure project management tools to send automated reminders:
- 48 hours before deadline: "Reminder: [Action Item] is due on [date]"
- On deadline day: "Due today: [Action Item]. Update status or flag blockers."
- 24 hours after missed deadline: "Overdue: [Action Item] was due [date]. Please update."

## 3. Internal: Three-Tiered Escalation

For critical data collection (compliance surveys, mandatory feedback, required sign-offs):

| Level | Timing | Method | Tone |
|-------|--------|--------|------|
| Level 1 | Shortly after initial deadline | Email reminder | Neutral, professional: "Friendly reminder that [item] is due" |
| Level 2 | 3-5 days after Level 1 | Direct phone/video call from coordinator | Supportive: "Wanted to check if you need any help completing [item]" |
| Level 3 | 5-7 days after Level 2 | One-on-one meeting or hard copy distribution | Assertive: "We need to resolve [item] — let's work through it together" |

Apply when:
- Compliance or regulatory requirements demand 100% completion
- Survey response rates fall below acceptable thresholds
- Sign-offs are blocking downstream work

## 4. External: Event "Pulse" Strategy

Key statistic: For every day follow-up is delayed, organizers experience an estimated 20% drop in prospect engagement.

### Six-Pulse Sequence

| Pulse | Day | Action | Channel | Key Principle |
|-------|-----|--------|---------|---------------|
| Pulse 1 | Day 1 (0-24 hrs) | Provide unconditional value: share recording, key resource, or relevant quote | Email (automated) | **Value first, no ask.** Build goodwill before requesting anything. |
| Pulse 2 | Day 2 | Ask for feedback via survey; frame around mutual benefit ("Help us improve the industry standard") | Email (automated) | **Feedback with purpose.** Explain the "why" to increase response rates. |
| Pulse 3 | Day 5 | Human-led personalized outreach to most engaged attendees (based on poll answers, Q&A, watch time) | Personal email, phone, or handwritten note | **Personalization.** Manual effort signals genuine interest. Prioritize by engagement score. |
| Pulse 4 | Day 12 | Community building content: share related article, announcement, or insight | Email (automated) | **Weekly value cadence begins.** Maintain presence without pressure. |
| Pulse 5 | Day 19 | Continued value delivery: share new resource, case study, or industry data | Email (automated) | **Sustained nurturing.** Keep the relationship warm. |
| Pulse 6 | Day 21+ | Analyze engagement data; identify prospects ready for formal next step (office visit, sales meeting) | Sales team review + personal outreach | **The "good ask."** Only request a meeting when data shows readiness. |

### Segmentation Rules

Segment attendees by behavior before applying cadence:
- **Attended live** → Full 6-pulse sequence
- **No-show (registered but absent)** → Modified: share recording (Pulse 1), then rejoin offer, then standard cadence
- **Watched on-demand** → Delayed start: begin at Pulse 2 (they already have the content)
- **Highly engaged (polls, Q&A, long watch time)** → Accelerate Pulse 3 to Day 3; prioritize for sales handoff

## 5. External: Sales & Deal Momentum Cadence

Key statistic: Effective follow-up is a key contributor in 40% of all closed-won deals. A significant portion of sales professionals prematurely abandon outreach after a single attempt.

### Standard 6-8 Touchpoint Cadence (2-3 weeks)

| Touchpoint | Day | Action | Channel | Key Principle |
|------------|-----|--------|---------|---------------|
| 1 — Initial | Day 1 | Specific recap of the discussion + clearly defined next step | Email | **Immediacy.** Send within 24 hours while conversation is top-of-mind. |
| 2 — Value Drop | Day 3 | Share relevant insight, case study, or resource related to their specific pain point | Email | **Persistence + value.** Never just "checking in" — always bring something useful. |
| 3 — Multi-channel | Day 8 | Touchpoint via different channel: LinkedIn connection/message, phone call, or video message | LinkedIn or Phone | **Channel diversification.** Different channels reach different attention windows. |
| 4 — New Resource | Day 15 | Deliver new resource: industry report, competitive analysis, or ROI calculator | Email | **Fresh value.** Provide material they haven't seen; slow down after a value drop. |
| 5 — Social Proof | Day 18 | Share a relevant customer success story or testimonial | Email or LinkedIn | **Credibility.** Let others make your case. |
| 6 — Final Check-In | Day 21+ | The "break-up" message: transparent closing ("If now isn't the right time, no problem") | Email | **Graceful closure.** Creates urgency without aggression; often triggers a response. |

### Cadence Rules

- Always pair persistence with value — each touchpoint offers something useful.
- Space out after a meaningful value drop (give them time to digest).
- Expand intervals progressively: Day 1 → Day 3 (2 days) → Day 8 (5 days) → Day 15 (7 days) → Day 21 (6 days).
- Customize content to the prospect's specific situation, pain points, and industry.
- Track engagement signals (email opens, link clicks, LinkedIn profile views) to prioritize follow-up intensity.
- After the "break-up" message, move to a quarterly nurture cadence (not monthly — avoid fatigue).

|-----------|----------------|-------------|---------------|
| Duration | 7 days | 21+ days | 21+ days |
| Touchpoints | 3 | 6 | 6-8 |

| Escalation | Review at next meeting | Sales handoff when ready | Break-up message as final step |

## 7. Operating Rhythms for Recurring Meetings

### Priority Tracker Pre-Work

Before each weekly check-in, team members update their tracker:

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| Accomplished | What was completed since last check-in |
| Up Next | What will be worked on this week |
| Blocked | What is preventing progress (needs leadership intervention) |

This 10-minute pre-meeting exercise transforms a status report meeting into a strategic problem-solving session.

### Weekly KPI Review Meeting Structure

1. Review company-wide KPIs and scorecards (10 min)
2. **Commitment follow-up**: Review action items from last week's meeting (5 min)
   - Completed items: acknowledge and close
   - In-progress items: confirm expected completion date
   - Overdue items: identify blockers and assign escalation
3. New issues and action items (10 min)
4. Priorities for the coming week (5 min)

### The 5-Minute Kick-Off

Start every recurring meeting with a 5-minute review of open action items from the previous session:
- Read each item and its DRI
- DRI provides a 15-second status update (Complete / On Track / Blocked)
- Capture new blockers immediately
- This creates social accountability — people complete tasks knowing they'll be asked publicly
